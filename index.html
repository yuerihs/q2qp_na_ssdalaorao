Organization Background

Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS), was founded by Muriel Jay, a British editor who was then residing in the Philippines in 1954. PAWS is an animal protection NGO run entirely by volunteers. The organization's primary tasks include educating the public and spreading knowledge about issues pertaining to animal care, welfare, and control.
In addition to strongly opposing events like dogfights and horse races as well as the usage of wild animals for entertainment, PAWS also actively campaigns against pet neglect and animal abuse.

Organization's Achievements

Paws Animal Rehabilitation Center (PARC)

PAWS was able to run an animal shelter for over 300 cats and dogs rescued from cruelty or neglect with the help of donations and volunteers. Here, these animals are rehabilitated in the hope of finding them new homes and a second chance at a good life. Additionally, there is also a free clinic that offers the impoverished population veterinary procedures, including spay and neuter (kapon).

Rabies Bill Passed; The Anti-Rabies Act, 2007 (Republic Act No. 9482).

After months of lobbying by DA, BAI, NMIS, and PAWS alongside many different vet groups and animal welfare NGOs, the Rabies Bill (or “an Act Providing for the Control and Elimination of Human and Animal Rabies, Prescribing Penalties for Violation Thereof and Appropriating Funds Therefor”) got its final reading at Congress last February 20, 2007.

Prizewinner of SPCA (HK) Canine Welfare Enhancement Award 2011

In recognition of the organization's tireless efforts in the field of animal welfare legislation, humane education, and policy development with respect to dog welfare, PAWS was given the first-ever SPCA (Hongkong) Canine Welfare Enhancement Award 2011 at the Gala Night of the 10th year of the Asia of Animals Conference in Kempinski Hotel, Chengdu, China last June 12, 2011
